Glitch One

Forgetting dy/dx when doing implicit differentiation.

Have you ever tried to plug in a USB? There's only two possible ways it can be plugged in but it always takes 3 tries right? It never seems to work regardless of whether the orientation is right or not. In the same way, people have a tendency to put a dy/dx where it shouldn't belong and forget to add a dy/dx when they actually need one.

Remember that stuff happens for a reason.

Glitch Two

Applying L'Hopital's Rule when the function isn't of indeterminate form.

When you don't fail on the hard questions, you'll probably fail on the easy problems. After seeing that the first problem of the test was a limit of the form 0/0 or infinity/infinity you realized immediately that you had to use L'Hopital's Rule. Good job! But unfortunately for you, all of the other limits weren't indeterminate but you applied L'Hopital's Rule for all of them without checking. Well, too bad. Either ask your teacher to stop trolling or just man up.
